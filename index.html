01 Introduction

CHRONIC ILLNESS

Chronic illness comes in many forms, and nobody is every truly prepared for what it can mean for them and those close to them. A member of my family was diagnosed with encephalitis, which is inflammation in the brain leading to cognitive decline and other health defects.

This diagnosis, as well as this project, led me to investigate the scale of chronic illness and what resources are available for those in need of care. Chronic illness isn't always something that can be prevented, but care after the diagnosis is even more important.

The following statistics show how widespread the effects of chronic illness can be, including those who become caregivers.

78% Family caregivers reporting burnout
63M Americans serving as family caregivers
70% Chance of needing long-term care for those turning 65
15% Annual Encephalitis rate per 100k people
02 Recursive Cascades

Recursive Cascades

Chronic conditions create what is called a "recursive cascade" where health vulnerabilities lead to social and financial crises, which lead to wrsening conditions and so on. For those with cognitive decline, the inability to manage their own needs often leads to them needing to move to assisted living, which are often less than ideal due to "slow abandonment" (see section 2). The recursive cascade of chronic illness is a symptom of the system itself, leading to many people with chronic illnesses having no options other than assisted living and Medicaid

"Chronic illnesses increase disadvantages for individuals and their households." — Manderson and Warren, Just One Thing after Another
03 Slow Abandonment

Slow Abandonment

Slow abandonment refers to the medical system leaving families alone to manage the effects of a chronic illness. As people grow older and more ill, and their care options become more expensive, many people end up having few options other than leaving their loved ones in assisted care facilities.

In America, many care facilities are deteriorating to a point where people will not stay there unless it is their only option. With the cost of long-term care being so high, many families are forced into these facilities where they do not have enough money to keep the place clean or properly pay their staff.

04 Resources

Resources

The purpose of this website isn't just to shed light on why chronic illness is so devastating to people, it is meant to inform and provide resources for those in need of help. Below are some resources for places to find help and information for you or a loved one who may need help.

National Institute of Neurological Brain Disorders and Stroke

The National Institute of Neurological Brain Disorders and Stroke is an informative source for understanding brain disorders, their symptoms, treatments, and the latest research.

Family Caregiver Alliance

The Family Caregiver Alliance specializes in supporting families caring for loved ones with chronic impairments. They excel at legal and financial planning as well as navigating the healthcare system.
